Yuxiang Eggplant

Description

This delectable Sichuan dish pairs fried-until-buttery eggplant slices with a complex sauce of tangy, spicy fermented chilis and deep umami Zhenjiang Vinegar (no, it does not in fact contain fish).

Ingredients

  • About 3 medium Chinese eggplants
  • 1 1/2 tbsp garlic
  • 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1 handful sliced scallion greens
  • 4 tsp fine sugar
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 3/4 tsp potato starch mixed with 1 tbsp cold water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp Sichuan Doubanjiang OR Sambal Oelek
  • 1 tbsp Zhenjiang black vinegar
  • 10 tbsp stock of choice
  • 1 pinch salt
  • Enough cooking oil to deep fry

Instructions

  1. Cut eggplants into batons about 7cm long. Mix well with salt, set aside for about 30 minutes.
  2. Rinse eggplant and dry thoroughly while heating oil in a wok to 200 degrees centigrade.
  3. Add eggplant to wok in moderate batches and fry until golden brown, about 3 minutes, then drain.
  4. Pour off all but 3 tbsp of oil from wok, then return to medium heat.
  5. Add bean paste or Sambal and fry untul oil is red. Add ginger and garlic, and stir fry until fragrant.
  6. Pour in stock, sugar and soy sauce, and bring to a boil.
  7. Add eggplant and stir in carefully. Simmer for a minute or two.
  8. Add potato starch to thicken, then vinegar and half the scallion greens. Stir for about 15 seconds.
  9. Plate with remaining scallion greens and Enjoy!
