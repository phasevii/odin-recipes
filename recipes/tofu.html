Mapo Tofu


Description

This famous dish of soft tofu and ground pork has become world famous as an exemplar of Sichuan cuisine's famous "ma-la" flavor, a hot and numbing profile that is among the world's most unique culinary experiences.

Ingredients

  • 500g plain white tofu
  • 2 scallions
  • 3 1/2 oz ground beef OR ground pork
  • 2 1/2 tbsp Sichuan Doubanjiang
  • 1 tbsp fermented black beans
  • 2 tsp ground chiles
  • 1 tbsp chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp chopped ginger
  • 3/4 cup stock of choice
  • 3/4 tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tbsp potato starch mixed with 2 1/2 tbsp cold water
  • 1/4 to 1 tsp roasted Sichuan pepper
  • 6 tbsp cooking oil of choice

Instructions

  1. Cut tofu into 3/4 inch cubes. Let steep in hot, lightly salted water while preparing aromatics, meat, and seasoning.
  2. Heat your wok over high heat. Pour in 1 tbsp cooking oil and heat until slightly smoking. Add beef and stir fry until completely cooked. Remove, drain, and set aside.
  3. Clean wok, then return to medium heat. Pour in remaining cooking oil and add doubanjiang, then black beans, then ground chili, stir frying each until fragrant and oil begins to turn red.
  4. Remove tofu from hot water, drain, and gently place into wok. sprinkle ground meat over top, then add stock and white pepper. Carefully nudge into sauce.
  5. Bring to boil, simmer for 2-3 minutes, then add potato starch mixture incrementally.
  6. Add scallions, pour into serving bowl, and enjoy!
