Dan Dan Noodles


Description

This dish originated as a form of street food sold by strolling vendors. It gets its name from their means of transporting it- long poles held across the shoulders. Its humble origins and simple appearance belie a lively, spicy dish that was enjoyed by the rich and poor alike, and continues to be both a local and international favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp sesame paste
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 tbsp Yibin yacai pickle
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 4 tbsp chile oil
  • 1 tsp Zhenjiang black vinegar
  • 2 tsp melted lard (optional)
  • 1/4 to 1/2 tsp ground roasted Sichuan pepper
  • 10 oz fresh OR 7 oz dried wheat noodles
  • 2 tbsp thinly sliced scallion greens

For the topping:

  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp chopped ginger
  • 3 1/2 oz ground pork
  • 1/2 tbsp Shaoxing wine
  • tsp sweet flour sauce
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp dark soy sauce
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. First, make the topping. Heat cooking oil in your wok over high heat. Add ginger and stir fry until fragrant, then add and brown the pork.
  2. Splash in Shaoxing wine, and cook pork until little moisture is left.
  3. Tilt the wok and push your pork to the side. Add sweet flour sauce to oil, fry, then stir pork back in and add both soy sauces. Stir and set aside.
  4. Dilute sesame paste with about 2 tbsp of oil from the wok, then re-oil and reheat the wok. Add yibin yacai and briefly fry. Divide sesame paste into two bowls, along with all other ingredients.
  5. Boil noodles to desired consistency, adding leafy greens to water to blanch.
  6. Drain noodles and greens, divide into bowls, then add pork topping.
  7. Mix noodles and greens with topping and enjoy!
